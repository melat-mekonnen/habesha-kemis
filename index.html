Description

(Amharic: ቀሚስ lit. "Shirt" or "Dress") is the traditional attire of Habesha women. The ankle-length dress is usually worn by Ethiopian and Eritrean women at formal events, holidays, and invitations, and comes in many regional varieties. It is made of cotton fabric and typically comes in white, grey, or beige shades. Many women also wrap a shawl called a netela around the formal dress.

Quality Ethiopian Habesha Kemis stands as a testament to excellence, intertwining pure cotton fabric and the artistry of skilled craftsmen. Crafted with precision and care, Habesha Kemis embodies a rich cultural heritage that spans generations. The exclusive use of high-quality cotton ensures comfort and durability, while the craftsmanship of skilled artisans infuses each garment with intricate detail and finesse. This harmonious blend of premium materials and expert artisanship culminates in Habesha Kemis, a symbol of quality, tradition, and timeless elegance.
